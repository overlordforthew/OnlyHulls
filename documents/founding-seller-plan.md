# Founding Seller Plan

Saved: 2026-04-16

## Current marketplace reality

- Active imported listings: `14,028`
- Active native seller listings: `0`
- Platform drafts: `3`
- Platform pending review: `0`
- Claim requests so far: `0`
- Live funnel events so far: `signup_created = 1`, `buyer_profile_saved = 1`

This means OnlyHulls is still an imported-inventory marketplace with almost no proof of live seller demand yet.

The seller acquisition plan should not pretend we already win on:

- audience scale
- lead volume
- time to sale

The plan should win on:

- no commission
- direct buyer contact
- sailboat and catamaran focus
- hands-on listing help
- claim-and-clean-up flow for boats already visible on OnlyHulls

## Core recommendation

Do not start with a broad "list on our marketplace" launch.

Start with a narrow wedge:

- private sailboat and catamaran sellers
- especially U.S., Florida, Caribbean, and Bahamas corridors
- owners who dislike broker commission and want help, not just software

Positioning should be:

- "Claim your boat and we will turn it into a seller-owned listing."
- "No commission."
- "Direct buyer contact."
- "We help clean up the listing for you."
- "Built for sailboats and catamarans, not generic marketplace clutter."

Do not lead with:

- "largest audience"
- "fastest sale"
- "AI-powered marketplace"

## 30-day wedge plan

### 1. Run a Founding Seller Program

Offer the first `25-50` sellers a free founding-seller listing.

The actual promise:

- we import the draft
- we help fix the specs, copy, and presentation
- we get the listing live with them
- they get direct buyer contact with no OnlyHulls commission

Ask for:

- responsiveness
- permission to use feedback/testimonials if they get value

### 2. Make claim flow the primary seller acquisition motion

The strongest acquisition path is not cold self-serve creation.

It is:

- seller sees their boat already on OnlyHulls
- seller clicks `Claim this listing`
- seller gets a prefilled draft
- OnlyHulls helps them tighten and publish it

This is the fastest bridge from imported inventory to native supply.

### 3. Use concierge onboarding at the beginning

Do not assume self-serve will carry the early supply side.

For early sellers:

- review every claimed draft manually
- request better photos if needed
- tighten title, summary, specs, and location
- push the seller to complete publish steps

The early goal is proof, not efficiency.

### 4. Sell help, not software

Early sellers do not care about product architecture.

They care about:

- presenting the boat well
- avoiding wasted time
- avoiding bad leads
- not paying commission

OnlyHulls should feel like:

- a focused sail/cat marketplace
- with founder-level help
- that gets the listing into shape
- and keeps contact direct

### 5. Build trust assets around seller outcomes

Seller-facing messaging should emphasize:

- no commission
- direct contact
- sailing-focused marketplace
- listing cleanup help
- cleaner presentation than generic marketplaces

### 6. Use outbound before paid acquisition

Do not spend meaningful money on ads first.

Start with direct outreach:

- sellers whose boats are already imported on OnlyHulls
- marina and sailing-community leads
- catamaran and liveaboard owner groups
- yards, surveyors, delivery captains, marine photographers, and local connectors

The goal is not massive top-of-funnel volume.

The goal is the first `10` strong native listings.

### 7. Delay aggressive paid seller monetization

Do not copy broad marketplace pricing too early.

Until seller demand and buyer response are proven, the better play is:

- free founding listings first
- optional premium upgrades later
- charge after proof, not before proof

## Success metrics for the first sprint

Judge the plan on seller activation, not just signups.

The first scoreboard should be:

- targeted seller conversations: `50`
- claimed or manually created drafts: `15`
- live native listings: `10`
- seller testimonials/case studies: `5`
- high-quality native listings with strong photos and copy: `80%+`

If OnlyHulls cannot get `10` live native listings with this much help, the problem is the offer, not traffic.

## Messaging summary

Best short version:

"Claim your boat on OnlyHulls, clean it up with us, and talk directly to buyers without paying commission."

## What to avoid

- claiming broader audience leadership before it is true
- positioning AI as the main seller hook
- charging too early without proof of value
- trying to win against generic boat marketplaces on scale
- treating brokers and private owners as the same motion

## Recommended next product/growth move

Run a `Founding Seller Claim Sprint`.

That means:

- stronger seller positioning on `/sell`
- stronger claim messaging on imported boat detail pages
- manual outreach to a hand-picked list of owners already represented in imported inventory
- high-touch onboarding until the first `10` seller-owned listings are live

This is the first seller acquisition plan that matches current marketplace reality and gives OnlyHulls a believable wedge.
